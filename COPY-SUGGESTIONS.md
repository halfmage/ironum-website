# Copy Analysis & Suggestions: Ironum Homepage & Key Pages
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Page Type:** Homepage (B2B SaaS / AI Services)
**Copy Score:** 56/100

---

## Executive Summary

Ironum's website copy is **technically competent but emotionally flat**. The site clearly communicates *what* Ironum does (AI services, GDPR compliance, European hosting) but fails to connect with *why a decision-maker should care right now*. The biggest gap: there is **no problem section** on the homepage — the copy jumps straight from "here's what we do" to service listings, skipping the critical step of making the visitor feel their current pain.

The headline "We Build AI Solutions. You Own the Results." is decent but generic — swap "AI Solutions" for any other tech category and it still works, which means it's not differentiated enough. The strongest copy is buried: the impact stats (40% lower costs, 70% faster compliance) and the 3-step process are compelling but appear below the fold, after walls of feature descriptions.

**Priority fixes:** (1) Rewrite the hero to lead with the prospect's problem, not Ironum's capabilities, (2) Move impact stats higher on the page, (3) Replace jargon (RAG, LLM, GEO) with outcome-oriented language in the hero, (4) Add a dedicated pain/problem section before services.

## Score Breakdown

### Clarity: 7/10
**Strengths:** The eyebrow "AI DEVELOPMENT PARTNER FOR EUROPE" is clear positioning. Service names are descriptive. The 3-step process is easy to follow.
**Weaknesses:** The H1 "We Build AI Solutions" is vague — "AI Solutions" means nothing specific. The subheadline lists "RAG systems, chatbots, and LLM fine-tuning" which are meaningless to a non-technical COO or Managing Partner. A first-time visitor needs 15-20 seconds to understand what Ironum actually delivers — should be 5 seconds.

### Persuasion: 5/10
**Strengths:** The "Free 30-min consultation · No commitment" line under the CTA is strong friction-reduction. The 3-step process (call → POC → production) is a good trust-builder.
**Weaknesses:** No problem/pain section anywhere on the homepage. No objection handling. No risk reversal beyond "free consultation." The "Why Ironum" section (Private, Customizable, Safe, Open) is generic — any AI company could claim these. No competitive framing.

### Specificity: 6/10
**Strengths:** Impact stats (40% lower costs, 70% faster compliance, 100% sovereignty) are excellent. "2 weeks" for POC is concrete. "400+ integrations" is specific.
**Weaknesses:** Hero copy has zero numbers or concrete outcomes. Service card descriptions are generic ("Technical audits, gap analysis" — what does the customer *get*?). "Trusted by European enterprises in finance, education and travel" — why not name them or give a number?

### Emotion: 4/10
**Strengths:** "You Own the Results" in the headline taps into ownership/control desire. The about page has a good founder story.
**Weaknesses:** No pain agitation anywhere. No "imagine what would happen if" aspiration. No urgency beyond passive mentions of EU AI Act. The copy reads like a capabilities brochure, not a conversation with a frustrated decision-maker. Missing: fear of falling behind competitors, frustration with US vendor lock-in, anxiety about compliance.

### Action: 6/10
**Strengths:** "Book Call With Gerrit" is personal and warm. Appears above the fold and bottom. Avatar image on the CTA button builds trust. "Free 30-min · No commitment" reduces friction.
**Weaknesses:** "Explore Our Platform" (Why Ironum section) is a weak CTA that leads to more browsing instead of conversion. "Learn more" on every service card is passive. No urgency mechanism. The newsletter CTA "Subscribe" is generic.

## Value Proposition Analysis

```
TARGET CUSTOMER: European mid-size enterprises (50-2000 employees)
PROBLEM: Want to use AI but blocked by GDPR/compliance concerns,
         fear of vendor lock-in, lack of technical expertise
SOLUTION: Full-service AI engineering — consulting through deployment
UNIQUE MECHANISM: European-hosted, open-source foundation, customer
                  owns all models/data/infrastructure
KEY BENEFIT: Enterprise AI without compliance risk or vendor dependency
PROOF: 4 client logos, impact stats (40%/70%/100%), 2-week POC promise
```

**Gaps identified:**
1. **PROBLEM is never stated on the homepage.** The visitor must infer it. This is the #1 copy issue.
2. **PROOF is thin.** Four logos with no context. No testimonials, no quotes, no named results. The stats section says "Based on measured outcomes" but doesn't attribute to specific clients.
3. **UNIQUE MECHANISM is buried.** The "European-hosted, open-source, you own everything" differentiator doesn't appear clearly until the Infrastructure section (4th section down).
4. **KEY BENEFIT lacks emotional weight.** "Without compliance risk" is rational. It needs an emotional layer: "So you can move fast on AI while your competitors are still stuck in legal review."
